BLOCBOX

Blocbox is a cloud-based information storage applicaton that leverages intuitive-based design principles to provide customers with a delightful, simple experience. It distinguishes itself from similar applications by providing user-centric navigational controls that can be accessed quickly and seamlessly.

DELIVERABLE

Responsive Webapp

SKILLS & RESPONSIBILITY

Sketch, Invision, Adobe CC

TOOLS & SOFTWARES

UX Design, UI Design, Brand Identity 

THE PROBLEM

Desigining a cloud-based storage SaaS that is highly intuitive and efficient

Most storage applications are highly linear in design and function. This leaves non-linear folks organizing in ways that are unatural for them. Balancing the linear/non-linear tension when designing a storage app is necessary if one aims to be user-centered. The real challenges lies in integrating both styles of processing information into an app that is clean, minimal and efficient.

THE SOLUTION

PROVIDING A USER-TESTED PRODUCT THAT DIFFERENTIATES ITSELF BY BEING HIGHLY INTUITIVE AND SIMPLE

Blocbox aims to resolve the lopsided linear nature of most storage apps by keeping the users natural tendencies at the forefront of the design process. The result is a a functional storage app that requires little effort to “figure out” and leaves users with a sense of utility and satisfaction.

THE PROCESS

Designing a delightful and beautiful product based on the key tendencies, wants and needs of Its users

From discovery to implementation the design process included many design tools and methods to ensure a storage product that is useful for both non-linear and linear types.

Discover & Strategize

RESEARCH

User Survey

Early in the design process, a survey was created in Google Drive that poses relevant questions about users’ behaviors, need for a product like Blocbox and demographics. Along with demographics, I wanted to see how users interact with their devices, share digital resources with others and how they login to online applications.

SURVEY SUMMARY

Respondents, all between the ages of 25 and 44, own a smartphone, tablet and laptop,  and prefer to browse online with their laptop when compared to other connected devices. Based on users stated needs, Blocbox had to include easy collaboration, notetaking, intuitive organization of digitial information and social media sign-in options. The most common ways that respondents saved content was by using browser bookmarkings. This finding allowed Blocbox to focus on to grouping content together to leverage the bookmarking style of content management that users already utilize.

Personas

Not satisfied with user research only, I created personas to empathize with the goals and behaviors of Blocbox users. This allowed me to finalize my requirements, create designs that solved problems that I wanted to address, validate my concepts and develop a script for usability tests.

USER STORIES

Using Google Drive I created user stories that aligned with the business requirements and my research. I leveraged the Pareto Principle - users will utilize 20% of the features 80% of the time - to create an MVP. Because I wanted to focus on the main tasks required for Blocbox users I listed tasks in order of importance. I also made a separate file for new users and returning users because they will navigate the application differently.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

Determining the strengths and weaknesses of applications that are aimed at similar users painted a clearer picture of the opportunities that Blocbox could capitalize on. Likewise, it clarified the specific hurdles that had to be jumped in order to be competitive in the marketplace.
  
There are many ways to analyze competitors. The tried-and-true SWOT analysis, with its focus on internal and external factors and its simplicity in targeting strategic action steps, continues to be my tool of choice.

Design

Architect & Prototype

User Flows

I transformed the research from my user stories to diagram in Draw.io how each group of users would interact with Blocbox. This series of user flows focused on the sequential process that users would follow, from beginning to end, when they were engaged with the application. 

I wanted new users to explore the features of Blocbox, sign-up using social logins or e-mail and password and navigate to the dashboard after signing-up. Likewise, I wanted returing users to be able to sign-in with their social logins or email and password and reset their password if desired. The other remaining user flows - account upgrades, dashboard and adding tags and favorites - would be shared by both groups.

Sitemap

View Sitemap

With my user flows completed I created a sitemap in Balsamiq to determine the specific pages of Blocbox. To keep an intuitive and simple navigation structure and hierarchy I utilized accepted usability standards, such as Hick’s law and the 3-Click Rule.

LOW-FIDELITY WIREFRAMES

In order to efficiently iterate navigational and structural components of the application I created low-fidelity wireframes in Balsamiq. This allowed for quick, early and cost effective tweaks for stakeholders. I quickly prototyped  the applicaiton with Invision and then employed Silverback 3, guerilla usability testing software, to record user interactions with Blocbox.

HIGH FIDELITY WIREFRAMES

With stakeholders’ approval high-fidelity wireframes were created using Sketch and Adobe Illustrator. Through consensus, the first homepage option was chosen. The professional-but-let’s-have-fun feel permeates through each page. With intuitively located calls to action Blocbox was getting closer to deployment.

TYPOGRAPHY, LOGOS & COLOR PALETTE

Typography, logos, and color palettes were also developed and iterated during the high-fidelity wireframing process.  After an agreed upon consensus we choose typography, color palette and logo 1.

PROTOTYPE

In order to have an interactive application for user testing I created a prototype with Invision. You can check out the PROTOTYPE if you would like. 

Using peek.usertesting.com and usabilityhub.com, I was able to create a five second test and nav flow test. Based on the results the brand came across as trustworthy, the design averaged four out of five stars and the Add button needed to be relocated based on a <90% success rate with the provided task.

DEPLOY

Implement

CODING

With Blocbox designed, tested and iterated I used Atom to develop the design into code, namely HTML, CSS and Javascript. Once completed I pushed the project to Github so that collaborators could access and edit it easily. Shortly after this point we had an MVP that was ready to go live.
Reflection and synthesis of the research, design and deployment of a product provides me with a synopsis of what was successful and how I/the team can be more excellent in areas where improvement can occur. The strengths of Blocbox lies in its intuitive and simple design. The main area of improvement is - of course not limited to -  a comprehensive feedback loop for post-deployment that allows the product to evolve and improve with its users. Overall the application is a great success that demonstrates the power of following a clear design process.
